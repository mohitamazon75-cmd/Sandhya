**Status:** Working name. Domain and trademark verification is the first task of next session. Fallback ranking: Nitya → Tithi → Smriti.

## 1. The Name

**Sandhya** (संध्या) — literally "the meeting." Traditionally the thrice-daily prayer juncture: pre-dawn, noon, dusk — the moments where day meets night meets day. The word names the threshold itself: the place where two states of being meet.

The name is the product. Every morning Sandhya is the meeting of the user with the day — what the day asks, what the tradition says, what a small practice might do. Every evening it is the meeting of the user with the day's residue — what happened, what was avoided, what to release before sleep. The product is structurally what the word means.

### 1.1 Why this name works

- **Two syllables.** Sand-hya. Universally pronounceable.
- **Spellable on first hearing.** Six letters. No ambiguity.
- **Authentic to the tradition.** A real Sanskrit word with a real religious meaning, in continuous use for thousands of years.
- **Already widely understood.** Most Indians know what *sandhya* means even without religious training. This is rare for a Sanskrit name; it lowers the explanation cost of the brand to almost zero.
- **Not in the astrology category.** No "Astro-," no "Vedic-," no "Jyotish-." We are not positioning as another astrology app.
- **Not in the wellness category.** No "Calm," no "Pause," no "Breathe." We are not positioning as another mindfulness app.
- **Translatable across languages without losing meaning.** Sandhya in Hindi, Sandhya in Tamil, Sandhya in Bengali. The Sanskrit root is the same.

### 1.2 Risks to verify next session

- Existing trademark in spiritual-tech / wellness / astrology in India (Indian trademark search).
- `sandhya.app`, `sandhya.com`, `sandhya.in`, `sandhya.co` availability.
- Existing common-name use as women's first name (very common — *not* a blocker, but check brand confusability).
- App Store / Play Store name conflicts.

If `.com` is taken (likely — common Indian first name), `.app` is acceptable and possibly preferable for our category. If both are taken or trademark search fails, fall back to **Nitya** (नित्य — "daily, eternal") as next-best.

## 3. One-Line Product Description

> Sandhya is a daily companion that reads each morning's sky against your exact birth chart and gives you what the tradition would say about the day — written warmly, anchored honestly, in the breadth of Sanatan thought rather than its narrow caricature.

When asked "what is it" in a sentence, this is the answer.

## 4. The Audience

The average Indian who opens WhatsApp first thing in the morning, plus the NRI variant of the same person. Ages 28–55. Reads English, Hinglish, or Hindi. Smartphone-native. Has tried mainstream astro apps and found them embarrassing. Has tried Western wellness apps and found them culturally hollow. Considers themselves spiritual but is past the bumper-sticker phase. Pays small monthly fees for things that fit into their existing morning rhythm rather than asking them to build a new one.

This is *not* the Bangalore seeker minority. It's the mass-market household-spirituality audience that the existing market either talks down to or scams.

## 5. The Wedge — Brand Commitment Form

Sandhya is the only daily Sanatan companion that holds all six together:

1. **Personal to your exact chart, not your sun-sign.**
2. **A whole morning, not a snippet.** Snapshot, story, principle, evening, sleep mantra — in one product.
3. **Written for adults, not for ad copy.** Editorial quality in Hindi and English.
4. **Refusal of dark patterns.** No streaks, no upsells, no fake urgency, no engineered fear.
5. **The full tradition, not the flattened version.** Multi-school, multi-regional, plural Sanatan.
6. **Beautiful and modern.** Designed in 2026, not 2008.

These six are the brand. Marketing copy can vary; the six commitments do not.

## 6. Anti-Positioning

What Sandhya is *not*. Stated explicitly so we never drift here:

- **Sandhya is not AstroTalk.** We do not sell astrologer consultations. We do not have a marketplace. We are not a supply business.
- **Sandhya is not Astrosage.** We are not a free utility for kundli generation. We do not optimise for SEO.
- **Sandhya is not Ganeshaspeaks.** We do not publish vague daily horoscopes for ad revenue.
- **Sandhya is not ClickAstro.** We are not a one-time-report business.
- **Sandhya is not Sri Mandir.** We do not sell virtual pujas.
- **Sandhya is not Calm or Headspace.** We are not a meditation app with Indian aesthetics painted on top.
- **Sandhya is not a guru personality cult.** No founder face, no hero figure, no "spiritual leader."
- **Sandhya is not a chatbot.** There is no "ask the AI" surface in v1. The composer is invisible infrastructure.
- **Sandhya is not a community.** No public feed, no comments, no likes. The relationship is between user and tradition, mediated by the product. Not user and other users.
- **Sandhya is not a horoscope.** The tradition says many things; "today is good for love" is not one of them.

When a feature, marketing decision, or positioning choice is on the table, the test is: does it move us toward any of the above? If yes, refuse it.

## 7. The Cultural Pluralism Commitment

Most Indian spiritual products default to North Indian Vaishnava-Vedanta because that is the version that exports easily. Sandhya commits, in writing, to the tradition's actual breadth:

- **Schools of philosophy that get rotated through the daily principle:** Advaita, Yoga (Patanjali), Bhakti (multiple lineages), Sant Mat (Kabir, Nanak-adjacent), Tantra-philosophical (Kashmir Shaivism, the philosophical Shakta), Samkhya, and the Upanishadic-Vedic ground common to all. Dvaita and Vishishtadvaita appear in v2 when we have the curatorial bandwidth.
- **Regions whose traditions get represented in stories and seasonal content:** North Indian Vaishnava and Shaiva; South Indian (Tamil Shaiva and Vaishnava traditions, Kannada Veerashaiva); Eastern (Bengali Shakta, Vaishnava); Western (Maharashtrian Bhakti, Gujarati); folk and tribal where ethically representable.
- **Voices in stories and principles:** men and women, householder and renunciate, scholar and folk, Sanskrit and vernacular. Akka Mahadevi and Mira sit beside Shankara and Aurobindo. (Bhakti voices arrive in v2 due to translation/license constraints; the commitment is on the record.)
- **Festivals respected:** the regional ones get their day. Onam, Pongal, Bihu, Durga Puja, Ganesh Chaturthi, Chhath, Vishu, Ugadi, Sankranti — not just the pan-Indian ones.

This commitment is what makes Sandhya genuinely different from every competitor. It is also the hardest thing about the product to execute. It requires real cultural literacy from the people building and editing it. The commitment is binding even when it is inconvenient.

## 8. Visual Identity (Skeleton)

Full visual identity is a separate document, produced in week 8 of the build during the polish phase. For now, the principles:

- **Typography respects Devanagari.** Hindi text uses a real Devanagari typeface, not a substitution font. (Recommended: Noto Sans Devanagari, Tiro Devanagari Hindi, or Mukti.)
- **Restrained colour palette.** Earth and ink. Saffron used sparingly and only when it earns its place. No clip-art deities, no gradient temples, no rainbow chakra graphics.
- **The morning message visual.** A single composed card. Information is layered, not stacked. The user sees one paragraph at first glance; details on tap-to-expand.
- **No stock photography of folded hands, sunsets over mountains, or "Indian-looking" models in saffron robes.** All visual content is original or specifically licensed.
- **Logo:** simple wordmark. *Sandhya* set in a typeface that respects both Latin and Devanagari script. No "lotus mark," no "om symbol," no diya icon. The name carries the brand.

The same voice rules from VOICE.md apply to marketing. Specifically:

- Landing page copy is written in the same Easwaran register as the product itself. No marketing-speak.
- Founder communication (Twitter, email, etc.) is honest about the small-product, careful-build positioning. We do not pretend to be larger than we are.
- We do not run fear-based marketing. We do not run urgency-based marketing. We do not run "limited time offer" promotions.
- We do not hire growth hackers. The product grows because users find it useful and tell their friends, or it does not grow.

## 10. Pricing as Brand

Pricing is a brand decision, not just a financial one. Sandhya's pricing communicates the positioning:

- **Free tier exists.** A user who cannot or will not pay still gets the morning snapshot. We do not gate the tradition behind a paywall.
- **Paid tier is small.** ₹149/month placeholder. Cheaper than a single AstroTalk consultation. The price says: this is for everyday use, not for crisis use.
- **No "premium consultation" tier.** No upsell beyond the basic paid tier. We are not a marketplace.
- **No discounts, sales, or promotional pricing.** The price is the price. Steady, predictable, dignified.
- **International tier is fairly priced for that audience.** ₹399 / $4.99 for NRI and non-Indian users. Same product, different currency.

## 11. The Founder Voice

When the founder writes publicly about Sandhya — Twitter, blog posts, podcast appearances, press — the consistent posture is:

- *Small and careful, not big and ambitious.*
- *Made by a believer, for everyday use, not as a tech demonstration.*
- *Honest about the tradition's gravity and our care in handling it.*
- *Quiet about the engineering.* The user does not care that we computed Vimshottari sub-periods to milliseconds. They care that the morning message lands.
- *Honest about what we are not.* We are not the biggest, the cheapest, the most-followed. We are trying to be the most trustworthy.

A reasonable description in the founder's own voice, for reference:

> "Sandhya is a small product I wanted for myself and couldn't find. I wanted a daily companion that took the tradition seriously without taking my money seriously, that was beautiful without being saccharine, that was personal to my chart without being a horoscope. I built it with care. It is not for everyone. If it is for you, you'll know within a week."
